BrainDock can notice activities during your session. For example, stepping away from your desk, distractions like using your phone, tablets, game controllers, etc. The best part is that it only flags these when you're actually using them in front of your screen, not just because they're nearby.

Screen sharing operates entirely offline, as it runs locally on your computer. AI-powered distraction sensing requires an internet connection to identify devices such as phones and tablets; however, no data is stored permanently on servers, and any data sent for analysis is discarded after processing.

Your session statistics, reports, and personal data remain on your computer. For distraction sensing, frames are temporarily transmitted to our AI models for analysis and then deleted. No data is stored permanently, and we do not retain records of your sessions.
